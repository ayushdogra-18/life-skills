# Questions and Answers on Listening and Communication

## Ques-1: What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? 
**Ans-1:**
- Avoid distractions by your own thoughts.
- Focus on the speaker and the topic also.
- Don't interrupt the speaker.
- Talk with the speaker using body language.
- If it's an important conversation, make short notes also.

## Ques-2: According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)
**Ans-2:**
1. **Listen carefully** – Pay full attention to the speaker. Don’t interrupt or think about your reply while they are talking.
2. **Understand the meaning** – Try to understand not only the words but also the feelings and ideas behind them.
3. **Check for accuracy** – Ask or confirm if your understanding is right, like “Did I understand you correctly?”
4. **Avoid judging or advising** – Don’t criticize, argue, or give solutions immediately. Focus only on understanding first.
5. **Encourage the speaker** – Use short responses like “I see,” “Go on.”

## Ques-3: What are the obstacles in your listening process?
**Ans-3:**
- Distractions
- Thinking about my reply
- Prejudgments
- Lack of interest
- Assumptions

## Ques-4: What can you do to improve your listening?
**Ans-4:**
To improve my listening, I can give my full attention to the speaker and avoid distractions like phones or noise. I should let the speaker finish without interrupting and focus on understanding their message instead of just planning my reply. Asking questions can help me clear doubts and make sure I understand correctly. I can also repeat what I heard in my own words to check if I got it right. Staying calm, open-minded. By practicing these habits, I can become a better listener.

## Ques-5: When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?
**Ans-5:**
I tend to use a passive communication style when I talk to new people. I also do this when I talk to seniors or elders.

## Ques-6: When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?
**Ans-6:**
I switch to an aggressive communication style when I feel very angry or frustrated. It usually happens if someone is being unfair, ignoring me, or not listening to my point of view. In those moments, I might speak loudly or strongly to make my opinion heard.

## Ques-7: When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day-to-day life?
**Ans-7:**
I sometimes become passive aggressive with friends, mainly through sarcasm or indirect comments. I usually do it in a playful way to build a closer bond and make the friendship more comfortable. However, it can be risky because sarcasm may be misunderstood, so I still try to keep it within limits and make sure I do not hurt anyone seriously.

## Ques-8: How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyze the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life? 
**Ans-8:**
- Explain my point clearly and directly.
- Use "I" statements like "I feel", "I expect", or "I need" instead of blaming.
- Clearly state what must be done and what should not be done.
 - Set boundaries and communicate expectations properly.
 - Stay calm and avoid being emotional or rude.
